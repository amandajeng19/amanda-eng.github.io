Part 1: Overview of the Dataset

The featured dataset includes video game sales data across various regions, genres, and publishers from 1980 to 2020.

1.1 Name

Type: Nominal

The title of the video game. This dataset lists 16,598 video games.

1.2 Platform

Type: Nominal/Categorical

The gaming platform on which the game was released (e.g. Wii, NES).

The platforms with the most games sold were DS (2,163) and PS2 (2,161). The platforms with the fewest games sold were GG (1), PCFX (1), TG16 (2), and 3DO (3).

1.3 Year

Type: Ordinal/Categorical

The year the game was released. The release dates span from 1980–2020. However, 2018 and 2019 are missing from the list.

Also, 271 games are listed as "N/A" for year of release. For this reason, these games will not be included in any year-based analyses.

1.4 Genre

Type: Nominal/Categorical

The genre of the video game (e.g. Sports, Platform, Racing).

Action games (3,316) were by far the top genre sold, followed by Sports (2,346). Puzzle (582) and Strategy (681) games were the least common genre.

1.5 Publisher

Type: Nominal/Categorical

The publisher of the game (e.g. Nintendo).

The top five game publishers were Electronic Arts (1,351), Activision (975), Namco Bandai Games (932), Ubisoft (921), and Konami (832).

Also, 203 games have an Unknown publisher, and 58 games are listed as "N/A." For this reason, these games will not be included in any publisher-based analyses.

1.6 Sales in North America

Type: Numerical/Quantitative

Total sales in the North America region (in millions of units).

The top five selling games in North America were as follows:

  1. Wii Sports (41.49 million copies)
  2. Super Mario Bros. (29.08 million copies)
  3. Duck Hunt (26.93 million copies)
  4. Tetris (23.2 million copies)
  5. Mario Kart Wii (15.85 million copies)

1.7 Sales in Europe

Type: Numerical/Quantitative

Total sales in the Europe region (in millions of units).

The top five selling games in Europe were as follows:

  1. Wii Sports (29.02 million copies)
  2. Mario Kart Wii (12.88 million copies)
  3. Wii Sports Resort (11.01 million copies)
  4. Nintendogs (11 million copies)
  5. Grand Theft Auto V (9.27 million copies)

1.8 Sales in Japan

Type: Numerical/Quantitative

Total sales in Japan (in millions of units).

The top five selling games in Japan were as follows:

  1. Pokemon Red/Pokemon Blue (10.22 million copies)
  2. Pokemon Gold/Pokemon Silver (7.2 million copies)
  3. Super Mario Bros. (6.81 million copies)
  4. New Super Mario Bros. (6.5 million copies)
  5. Pokemon Diamond/Pokemon Pearl (6.04 million copies)

1.9 Sales in Other Regions

Type: Numerical/Quantitative

Total sales in other regions (in millions of units).

The top five selling games in other regions were as follows:

  1. Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas (10.57 million copies)
  2. Wii Sports (8.46 million copies)
  3. Gran Turismo 4 (7.53 million copies)
  4. Grand Theft Auto V (4.14 million copies)
  5. Mario Kart Wii (3.31 million copies)

1.10 Global Sales

Type: Numerical/Quantitative

Total global sales (in millions of units).

The top five selling games globally were as follows:

  1. Wii Sports (82.74 million copies)
  2. Super Mario Bros. (40.24 million copies)
  3. Mario Kart Wii (35.82 million copies)
  4. Wii Sports Resort (33 million copies)
  5. Pokemon Red/Pokemon Blue (31.37 million copies)
