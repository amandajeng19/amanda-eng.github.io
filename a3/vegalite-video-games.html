Part 1: Overview of the Dataset

The featured dataset includes video game sales data across various regions, genres, and publishers from 1980 to 2020.

1.1 Name

Type: Nominal

The title of the video game. This dataset includes 16,598 video game entries.

1.2 Platform

Type: Nominal/Categorical

The gaming platform on which the game was released (e.g. Wii, NES).

The platforms with the most games sold were DS (2,163) and PS2 (2,161). The platforms with the fewest games sold were GG (1), PCFX (1), TG16 (2), and 3DO (3).

1.3 Year

Type: Ordinal/Categorical

The year the game was released. The release dates span from 1980–2020. However, 2018 and 2019 are missing from the list.

Also, 271 games are listed as "N/A" for year of release. For this reason, these games will not be included in any year-based analyses.

1.4 Genre

Type: Nominal/Categorical

The genre of the video game (e.g. Sports, Platform, Racing). Note that 1,739 games are listed under Misc.

Action games (3,316) were by far the top genre sold, followed by Sports (2,346). Puzzle (582) and Strategy (681) games were the least common genre.

Puzzle and Strategy are my favourite game types — I enjoy solving logic and word puzzles. I am fairly introverted, and I wonder if I prefer this gaming style because it’s usually more independent/solitary.

1.5 Publisher

Type: Nominal/Categorical

The publisher of the game (e.g. Nintendo).

The top five game publishers were Electronic Arts (1,351), Activision (975), Namco Bandai Games (932), Ubisoft (921), and Konami (832).

Also, 203 games have an Unknown publisher, and 58 games are listed as "N/A." For this reason, these games will not be included in any publisher-based analyses.

Part 2: Visualization Explorations

2.1 Global Sales by Genre and Platform

Question A: Which video game genres had the highest sales globally?

The Action and Sports genres had the top sales, and the Puzzle, Adventure, and Strategy genres had the fewest.

It was interesting to compare this chart to the distribution of games by genre (Section 1.4):

  • Lots of Adventure games were released, but they only sold moderately in quantity.
  • A moderate number of games were listed as Miscellaneous, but they’re the third-most category for global sales. Perhaps uniqueness has a correlation with popularity.

Question B: Which gaming platforms had the highest sales globally?

The PS2 had by far the highest game sales, followed by Xbox360 and PS3.

Doing a similar comparison with the game distribution by platform (Section 1.2), I noticed:

  • The quantity of games released for PS2 and DS are similarly high, yet actual sales for PS2 games was significantly higher.

2.2 Sales Over Time by Platform and Genre

Question A: What were the peak sale years and trends for the top-selling gaming platforms?

The peak sale years for a few notable games were as follows:

  • PS: 1998
  • PS2: 2002 & 2004
  • DS: 2005, 2007, & 2008
  • Xbox360: 2008 & 2010
  • Wii: 2008 & 2009
  • PS3: 2011
  • PS4: 2015

These results don't really surprise me — I remember the Wii versus Xbox preference debates when I was in elementary school around 2008–2010.

Also, no matter how big, most platforms drop off about 5–10 years after their initial release. I suppose the hype for them dies down, and the next big concole comes along.

Question B: What were the peak sale years and trends for the top-selling genres?

The top selling genres (Action, Sports) didn't really reach any peaks until the early-mid 2000s. For example, Sports game sales more than doubled between 2005 and 2006, while Action games had a major peak in 2002 and a bigger peak in 2008–2009. I’m guessing games in this games required a lot of dexterity and quick reaction times, and it took the passing of the 21st century for vido game technology to reach that level.

Meanwhile, Puzzle games and Platformers had minor peaks all through the 1980–2020 period yet remained somewhat low/consistent, never reaching the levels that the top genres did. I think these ones require more thoughtfulness and mental problem solving to play, so the improvements in technology don't make as much of a difference.

2.3 Regional Sales versus Platform

Question: Which platforms sold the best in each region?

Some key findings from the chart include the following:

  • Wii games were extremely popular in North America (41.49 million) and Europe (29.02 million), but only moderately popular in Japan and other regions.
  • GB and NES both performed well in Japan and North America.
